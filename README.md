# Customer Segmentation using Unsupervised Learning

### 1. Project Background

``` Not all customers are made equal```

According to a 2025 survey by [Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-05-12-gartner-2025-cmo-spend-survey-reveals-marketing-budgets-have-flatlined-at-seven-percent-of-overall-company-revenue), most companies spend approximately 7-9% of their annual revenue on marketing. Given the budgets, marketing teams are under pressure to guarantee a high ROI, and the first mistake they should be avoiding is treating every customer the same.They therefore need deep insights and visibility about who their customers are and the best way to engage them to close a sale.

Customer segmentation is a critical strategy in modern business analytics that enables companies to understand and categorize their customer base. It involves breaking down a customer base into distinct groups with similar characteristics- such as spending habits or product interests. This allows businesses to, instead of lauching blanket marketing campaigns they can tailor their marketing strategies, product offerings, and services to meet the specific needs of different customer segments.

Traditionally, customer segmentation was a tedious process that relied on predefined business rules or demographic categories, like ***'customers between the age of 25-35'***, ***'customers who spent KES 2,000 in the last month'***. However, unsupervised machine learning techniques, particularly clustering algorithms, offer a data-driven alternative that can uncover hidden patterns and natural groupings within customer data that might not be immediately obvious to human analysts.

In this project, I employed unsupervised clustering methods to segment customers, in my hypothetical store called 'Wamz General Stores' . The main aim of this project is to use transaction data to identify customer clusters, conduct cluster analysis to uncover similar charcateristics and finally recommend enagement strategies for the different customer clusters.
